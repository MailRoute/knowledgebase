This guide contains information for the Administrators of the MailRoute Email Protection Service.  It contains detailed information on setting up your account, managing domains, users and settings.  It assumes that you are familiar with administering email services for an organization.

### Concepts

#### Organization

MailRoute's organizational structure follows this pattern:

        Customer
          Domain
            Email Account
        
A **Customer** has one or more **Domains**.  A **Domain** has one or more **Email Accounts** associated with it.

As a **Customer Admin**, you're in control of all your domains and all your users.  An admin can add or remove domains and change their email delivery settings, white and blacklists and default filter and notification settings.  They can add or remove users, set their per-user settings, and view their individual email quarantines.  You can make all these changes through the MailRoute Control Panel, or via our [API](https://support.mailroute.net/forums/21682281-API).


### The MailRoute Control Panel


#### Logging In

Once you've signed up for the MailRoute Email Protection Service, you're provided with an administrative login for the MailRoute Control Panel via email.  Visit [https://admin.mailroute.net](https://admin.mailroute.net/) and login with your email address and password.

![Login](http://static.mailroute.net/kb/login.png)


#### Navigating the Control Panel

The tabs at the top of the **Control Panel** provide quick access to all your **Customers**, **Domains**, and **Email Accounts**.  

![MailRoute Control Panel](http://static.mailroute.net/kb/admin.png)

In addition, if your login is also within a domain that is filtered by MailRoute, you can access your own personal Quarantine and your individual user settings.

![Admin Quarantine](http://static.mailroute.net/kb/admin_qtn.png)
![Admin Personal Settings](http://static.mailroute.net/kb/admin_personal_settings.png)
