## Domain-wide Whitelists and Blacklists

As an administrator, you can add whitelist or blacklist entries that apply to every user in your domain.  Individual user settings override these domain-wide settings - if you blacklist "hotmail.com", but an enduser whitelists "user@hotmail.com" or even all of "hotmail.com", then their settings will override the domain-wide settings.

## White and Blacklists

Whitelisting and blacklisting allows or blocks messages sent by specific senders.  Please be aware that email senders can be forged - you can't rely on the sender 100%.

Blacklisted senders are blocked and end up in the quarantine.

Whitelisted senders always get past the spam filters, but if their messages are corrupt or virus infected, our other filters may capture them, in order to keep you well-protected.

![Whitelist](http://static.mailroute.net/kb/whitelist.png)

Add indivdual senders or domains with the "Add Whitelist" or "Add Blacklist" buttons.  Or enter many at once with the "Bulk Whitelist..." option in the "More Actions" menu.

![Whitelist](http://static.mailroute.net/kb/whitelist_bulk.png)

You can white or blacklist exact email addresses like **user@domain.com** or entire domains such as **test.com**.

The more-specific entries override the less-specific, so you can blacklist **hotmail.com**, but exempt your grandma by whitelisting **grandma@hotmail.com**
