### Email Filtering Marketplace
	
	
#### Generic Hardware Solutions

* Costly, due to hardware and labor 
* Doesn't reduce load on customers' servers
* Requires  upgrades and additional systems
* Time-consuming and labor-intensive
* Maintenance and downtime
* Single point of failure unless customer builds-in expensive redundancy


####Cloud Solutions
The marketplace is changing significantly. Google acquired Postini and is wrapping it into Google Apps, no longer renewing Postini contracts after October 31, 2012. This leaves 70 million Postini users looking for a cloud-based email filtering service replacement. 

Microsoft bought FrontBridge, founded by MailRoute's CEO Tom Johnson, and is now wrapping their Forefront (FOPE) product into Office 365. Many businesses choose not to operate their companies exclusively through the cloud.

There are a few players in MailRoute's space, and none offer MailRoute's reliability or technology.

#### Webroot, the cautionary tale
This company offered cloud-based spam and virus filtering, but did not own their technology vertically. The costs of running the service on systems that were not their own proved to be fiscally untenable and they've opted to sell filtering software instead of a cloud service.
